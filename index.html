About me:

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the VIB-KU Leuven Center for Microbiology, currently studying the functionality of the gut microbiota.

During my PhD studies I focused my research on a single bacterium, Mycoplasma pneumoniae. I studied the different mechanisms that this bacterium uses to regulate transcription, from canonical transcription factors to alternative mechanisms such as small RNAs or riboswitches. I analyzed hundreds of datasets from different omics, mostly RNA-seq and ChIP-seq.

After my PhD, I became interested in how bacteria behave in community rather than in isolation. My current research involves profiling the (meta-)transcriptome of gut communities in order to understand the microbial interactions occurring in this specific ecosystem.

Since November 2017, I also organize the local R-Ladies chapter in Leuven, Belgium (https://www.meetup.com/rladies-leuven/).
