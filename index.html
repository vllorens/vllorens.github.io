About me:

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the VIB-KU Leuven Center for Microbiology, currently studying the functionality of the gut microbiota.

During my PhD studies I focused my research on a single bacterium, Mycoplasma pneumoniae. I studied the different mechanisms that this bacterium uses to regulate transcription, from canonical transcription factors to alternative mechanisms such as small RNAs or riboswitches. I analyzed hundreds of datasets from different omics, mostly RNA-seq and ChIP-seq.

After my PhD, I became interested in how bacteria behave in community rather than in isolation. My current research involves profiling the (meta-)transcriptome of gut communities in order to understand the microbial interactions occurring in this specific ecosystem. Besides, I am developing both experimental and computational tools to profile the metatranscriptome of host-associated microbial communities.

In January 2023, I will join the Centro de Investigación Príncipe Felipe as a Junior Group Leader, after obtaining a highly competitive Ramón y Cajal fellowship. I will study the importance of bacterial gene regulation in determining how bacteria from the gut microbiome interact with their abiotic environment, other microbes, and the host.
